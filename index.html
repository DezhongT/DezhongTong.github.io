I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering at the University of California, Los Angeles. I have the privilege of being a member of the Structure-Computer Interaction Laboratory at UCLA and being supervised by Professor M. Khalid Jawed. Prior to my time at UCLA, I got my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

My research is driven by the desire to integrate physics, machine learning and robotics to create sim2real control schemes for robots that are informed by physics, and real2sim to explore complex physics phenomena. Specifically, my research interests includes:

  • Computer graphics for simulating physical systems with large geometric nonlinearity(deformable objects),
  • Robotic manipulation of deformable objects such as rods and shells,
  • Non-smooth mechanics for simulating frictional contact and interesting discontinuous process (buckling) in slender structures
